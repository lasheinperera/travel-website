Arugam Bay

Arugam Bay is a coastal village located on the eastern coast of Sri Lanka. It is known for its stunning beaches, world-class surf breaks, and laid-back vibe.

Buddha Statue at Gangarama Temple

Gangaramaya Temple is a Buddhist temple located in the heart of Colombo, Sri Lanka. It is known for its intricate architecture, impressive collection of Buddha statues and artifacts, and serene surroundings.

Mirissa Beach

Mirissa Beach is a picturesque beach town located on the southern coast of Sri Lanka. It is famous for its stunning sunsets, long sandy beach, and whale watching tours that offer visitors the chance to see blue whales and dolphins in their natural habitat.

Stilt Fishing

A traditional fishing method practiced in Sri Lanka for generations. Fishermen perch on stilts in the shallow waters along the coast, using simple fishing rods and lines to catch small fish, often during sunrise and sunset, creating a unique and iconic image of Sri Lanka's coastal heritage.

Dunhinda Waterfall

Dunhinda waterfall is a majestic waterfall located near the city of Badulla in Sri Lanka. The waterfall is around 64 meters high and is surrounded by lush greenery, making it a popular spot for nature lovers and hikers.

Lotus Tower

Lotus Tower is a telecommunications tower located in Colombo, Sri Lanka. Standing at a height of 350 meters, it is the tallest structure in South Asia and the 11th tallest tower in the world. The tower's design is inspired by the lotus flower, which is considered a symbol of purity and prosperity in Sri Lankan culture.

Light House at Galle Fort

The lighthouse in Galle Fort is a historic landmark located in the southern city of Galle, Sri Lanka. Built-in 1939, the lighthouse stands tall at 26.5 meters and is a popular attraction for tourists who visit Galle Fort to experience its rich history and picturesque surroundings.

Nine Arches Bridge

The Nine Arches Bridge is a historic railway bridge located in the town of Ella, Sri Lanka. Built-in 1921, the bridge spans a picturesque valley and is known for its unique architecture and stunning views, attracting visitors from all over the world.

Petta Streets

Pettah is a bustling neighborhood located in the heart of Colombo, Sri Lanka. Known for its vibrant markets, colorful streets, and diverse cultural influences, Pettah is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the sights, sounds, and flavors of Sri Lanka.

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage is a sanctuary for orphaned and injured elephants located in the town of Pinnawala, Sri Lanka. The orphanage is home to a large herd of elephants, and visitors can observe them up close, feed them, and even watch them bathe in the nearby river.

Sigiriya

Sigiriya is an ancient rock fortress located in the northern Matale District of Sri Lanka. The fortress was built in the 5th century and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its stunning frescoes, intricate water gardens, and impressive rock formations.

Arugam Bay

Arugam Bay is a coastal village located on the eastern coast of Sri Lanka. It is known for its stunning beaches, world-class surf breaks, and laid-back vibe.

Gal Viharaya

Gal Viharaya is a collection of four ancient Buddha statues carved into a large granite rock face located in Polonnaruwa, Sri Lanka. The statues, which date back to the 12th century, are considered masterpieces of ancient Sinhalese Buddhist art and attract visitors from all over the world.

Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna Beach is a beautiful crescent-shaped beach located in the southern part of Sri Lanka. Known for its crystal-clear waters, golden sand, and coral reefs, the beach is a popular spot for swimming, snorkeling, and diving.

Dutch Fort

The Dutch Fort in Galle is a historic fortress located in the southern city of Galle, Sri Lanka. Built by the Portuguese in the 16th century and later expanded by the Dutch, the fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its colonial architecture, beautiful views of the Indian Ocean, and rich history.

Tea Estates

Tea estates are an iconic feature of the Sri Lankan landscape, with rolling hills and lush green tea bushes stretching as far as the eye can see. Sri Lanka is one of the world's leading tea-producing countries, and visitors can explore the tea estates, learn about the tea-making process, and sample some of the finest teas in the world.

Mt.Lavinia Beach

Mount Lavinia Beach is a popular beach located in the southern part of Colombo, Sri Lanka. The beach is known for its golden sands, clear waters, and lively atmosphere, making it a favorite spot for both locals and tourists to relax, swim, and enjoy water sports.

Somawathiya Temple

Somawathiya Temple is an ancient Buddhist temple located in the northeastern part of Sri Lanka. The temple is believed to be one of the sixteen sacred places visited by the Buddha, and it is known for its magnificent stupa, beautiful murals, and serene surroundings.

Yala National Park

Yala National Park is the most visited and second-largest national park in Sri Lanka, located in the southeastern part of the country. The park is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, leopards, crocodiles, and many species of birds, making it a popular destination for wildlife safaris and nature lovers.

Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya is a scenic hill town located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. Known as "Little England," it features colonial-style architecture, lush tea plantations, and a cooler climate, making it a popular destination for those seeking a break from the heat and to enjoy the beautiful scenery.
